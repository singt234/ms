Impact Assessment

CC Lecture

Nothing exists or is made in a vacuum. This final prompt asks you to reflect on the impact of your work this semester along the following vectors:

Audience:What will exposure to your project have on its audience?
Community of Practice:How will your work impact the community of practice in which you are situated?
Environment:What impact on the specific and broader environment? This should take into account the impacts of materials, tools, output, etc.
Cultural Context:How will your project situate in and influence the broader culture within which it is situated?

- My least successful prototypes were:

Prototype - IV and Prototype - VII



- How did it fall short?

The biggest issue with these prototypes is they are more like concepts. No physical/actual testing has been conducted. It would be really great if I can test them out and see how people are liking the concept and so that I can build them into a prototype to test out with people.



- Why did it fall short?

The focus was not ideal. I spent too much time coming up with a strong concept, finalizing it but never got to execute it. The prototypes eventually just become a writeup/plan/concept without proper testing or execution. It would be great if I could create a proper prototype and test them out.



- What could you have done differently?

Definitely focusing on testing the prototype and get feedback instead of trying to perfect the writeup or concept. I could have tried some paper prototyping and test them with the users.



- What do these moments of failure suggest about future directions for your Final Project?

It is important to get out there and try to get results or feedback from others instead of trying to run through everything in my mind.
I would be trying to get as much feedbacks as I can. Even on the concepts, I could get the feedback on concept that can help me to build the stronger prototype.
