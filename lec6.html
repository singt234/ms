Discover, Scale, Name, Narrate, Problematize

CC Lecture

This week’s prompt is about finding ideas for a project emerging from your initial research, your creative intentions, and your overall goals for MFADT. Use the following prompts to seek out opportunities for project-based continuations of your research. Try to write against at least three of these.

Discover: Look through different instruments or lenses at the PoV you’ve established around your topic.
Scale: Amplify, Expand or Condense components/phenomena/actions already present. What happens if you scale your ideas up or down?
Name: Determine an individual member of a specific public and work just for them.
Narrate: Determine actors and plot points and construct a story...and fill in the parts that are missing. Consider myth, drama, mystery, comedy—fiction can be as strong as fact.
Problematize: Instead of solving a problem, make a problem/challenge visible and physical

1. Discover:

So, in the last prompt, I discovered my research question. My research question is how to make searching place, rooms, houses easy for every user?



2. Narrative/Problematize:

So, when I got accepted at parsons, one of the crucial task is to find a place to live. I started looking for accommodations before coming to New York. I did what every person would do. Contact friends, friends of friends, joined facebook groups, made account on every possible website, which helps in finding room.
But no luck. It's not that I didn't like any of the listings. I contacted many people and got a reply from some of them. But they were all scams. People were sending me phishing pages, asking for a security deposit without me looking at the space.
So I decided to book an Airbnb for a month and continue my search in the meantime. After reaching here, I went to my Airbnb, and what I got the place was different from the pictures and amenities mentioned were not there.



3. Name:

My problem is targeted to a specific audience. My particular audience is international students who go through this problem. I interviewed many international students and got to know about their side. What challenges did they face? How did they overcome them? What kind of solution are they looking for?



4. Scale:

I want to expand my specific question to all the people looking for a space. Not only rooms or only for international students, but in a broader context, I would like to make it easy for every user who is looking for any space. Whether it's a businessman looking for a space to organize events or conferences or a person shifting to another state or country because of job.
