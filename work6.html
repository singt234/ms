Personal Reflection

Major Studio I

I am more explicit about the idea and problem after the feedback from the critics. To be very real, I was focusing on the things I wanted to focus on. There were some things that people I interviewed mentioned in their interview. I picked out the things I wanted to listen or hear. But, If I explore that other side/things, maybe I should get something more exciting and innovative.

After reading the article "Changing Perspective," I realized that I am precisely doing the things that I shouldn't be doing. Also, talking to Harpreet, after the presentation that day, he told me the same things.
And after reading the articles and feedback from the critics. I was asking question to myself, why companies like Airbnb haven't tried technologies like Augmented Reality and Virtually Reality on their sites?
It's not like they haven't thought of it yet. Then what can be the problem? Is it about the experience of the user? Or it won't help the user in any way? But from the point I am thinking, is like 3D experience gives better insights as compared to 2D images. But maybe not in this case. Perhaps it won't solve any problem here?

After the feedback, I also give a thought about the problem! I am trying to solve so many problems. Maybe I should focus on one at a time? Should I focus on solving the trust problems or help them visualizing better? What is the major challenge faced by international students? How can I provide them the insights and information or internal network of the place they are going? In my case: New york.

I checked listingsProject by Stephanie Diamond, as mentioned in feedback, and I tried to understand the way they are solving the problem. In Listing Project, they are sending the list of available spaces, like a weekly newsletter. But how they are getting the listings? How people get to know about them? How people coming to New York, or the places they have listings for will get to know about them? These were some of the questions I was thinking of when I was reading about them.

After all this, I am researching the offline way to solve this problem, like building the community and making connections between people so that may help people to find the place. I am not sure how to pursue this or what things to keep in mind. I am exploring the ways/ideas that can be done to solve this problem — not thinking about the technology anymore, focusing on the issue of building trust among people. How can I make people trust that listing? How people used to find places when there were no websites/technology?

I thought about the 90's time, how did you get to know about the place where we are living right now? Have you ever used the website to find the site? How were the times when there were so less real estates or brokers were there? How my grandfather used to find the place? People were putting the board/big posters on the property, mentioning this space available for rent with a phone number of the person to contact.

Moreover, I am trying to connect with Stephanie Diamond to know the thought process behind the ListingsProject. What were the problems she was facing? How is she getting the listings? How she came up with this idea? Also, I thought about whether this kind of approach will solve my problem or not? I am going to research more on the things done in the past and present to make the future.
